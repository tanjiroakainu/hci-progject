The Tragic Event

On April 14, 1865, just days after the end of the Civil War, Abraham Lincoln was assassinated by John Wilkes Booth, a Confederate sympathizer, at Ford's Theatre in Washington, D.C.

Details of the Assassination

Lincoln was attending a play titled Our American Cousin with his wife, Mary Todd Lincoln, when Booth entered the presidential box and shot him in the back of the head. Lincoln was taken to a nearby boarding house, where he passed away the following morning.

Aftermath

Booth fled the scene but was tracked down and killed 12 days later. Lincoln's death plunged the nation into mourning, and his funeral procession was attended by thousands of grieving Americans.

Illinois Legislature

Lincoln's political career began when he was elected to the Illinois state legislature as a member of the Whig Party in 1834. He served four successive terms in the Illinois House of Representatives.

U.S. House of Representatives

In 1846, Lincoln was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives, where he served one two-year term. During his time in Congress, he opposed the Mexican-American War and proposed the "Spot Resolutions," which questioned President Polk's claims about the start of the war.

Lincoln-Douglas Debates

In 1858, Lincoln ran for the U.S. Senate against Stephen A. Douglas. The famous Lincoln-Douglas debates took place during this campaign, with slavery being the primary topic. Although Lincoln lost the election, these debates elevated his national profile.

Republican Nomination and Presidential Election

In 1860, Lincoln secured the Republican Party nomination for president. He went on to win the presidential election, defeating three other candidates in a divided nation. His victory without a single electoral vote from the South triggered the secession crisis.
